Viet Eating Culture

Are you ready for a food trip to Vietnam?

Breakfast is a must!

Vietnamese always make breaskfast a big deal. It is an important meal that enpower them until lunch. Eating out is extremely common in Vietnam because food can be easily accessed fromt he food stands on the street. There are a variety of delicious food you can get for breakfast: Banh mi (sandswitch), Bun bo hue (Hue's beef noodle), Com suon (rice with BBQ pork), Xoi (sticky rice with sliced sauce).

Dusty Lunch

Eating lunch is sometimes called com bui (disty rice). As its name suggests, vietnamse convinient ly grab some food from a nearby food place on the street. Lunch needs to be quick because, in vietnamese culture, the longer they eat the shorter their one hour break can be used for a quick nap.

Fish Sauce

Dinner is a big meal. Family members gather in a commuting dinner. Dinner is usually prepared by the women of the family. It consist a couple of dishses shared by everyone. Each has their own bowl of rice. The dishes can be many thing, but there must be at least one dish from each of these cagory: soup, braised, stir. It is completely acceptable to raise your bowl of rice up to your mouth and use chopsticks to scoop your the food into your mouth. Vietnamese don't serve deserts but fruit comes after the meal.
