    Tết ([tet˧˥] or [tɜːt˧˥]), or Vietnamese New Year, is the most important celebration in Vietnamese culture. The word is a shortened form of Tết Nguyên Đán, which is Sino-Vietnamese for "Feast of the First Morning of the First Day". Tết celebrates the arrival of spring based on the Vietnamese calendar, which usually has the date falling in January or February.[1]

    New Year Eve Party

    Just like everywhere in the world, Vietnamese also celebrates the completion of the year on New Year Eve. This is a rare opportunity for family reunion. Family members, who live in different parts of the country, gather in the parents' house and shares their stories as they enjoy full-course dinner.

    Dried fruits and nuts for guests on the 1st.

    It is customary to visit your neighbors during the first day of the new year. However, some family are very selective about the first person of the year who enters their house, so be aware if you are planning to visit someone as their firs guest. As a part of holiday custom, you will be welcome with "Mut", dried fruits and nut. Some of the common dried fruits often include coconut, pineapple, star fruit and watermelon seeds and candied ginger.

    Braised Pork with eggs on the 3rd

    Vietname typically don't cook during the first three days of new year. Food for the entire holiday season has been prepared upfront, and one of them is a big pot of braised pork with eggs, which feeds the Vietnamese family in the rest of the holiday season, from the 3rd to the 6th of January.
