    Carps on the Kitchen God day.

    In Vietnamese tradition, kitchens are guarded by Kitchen Gods, who will look over the house for the owner. On December 23, it is believed that Kitchens Gods go up to the sky to present their end of year report to the God of the Sky. Interesting enough, riding on carps Is the only way for Kitchen Gods to go up to the sky. Therefore, it is no surprise to see carps somewhere in Vietnamese family on December 23.

    New Year Eve Party

    Just like everywhere else in the world, Vietnam also celebrates the completion of the year on New Year Eve. This is a rare opportunity for family reunion. Family members, who live in different parts of the country, gather in the parents' house and shares their stories as they enjoy full-course dinner. After the dinner, everyone counts down for the new year celebration.

    Dried fruits and nuts for precious guests on the 1st.

    It is customary to visit your neighbors during the first day of new year. However, some families are very selective about the first person of the year who enters their house, so be aware if you are planning to visit someone as their first guest. As a part of the holiday customs, you are expected to talk pleasantly, and swearing the strictly prohibited since it's a sign of bad luck. In return, you will be welcome with "Mut", dried fruits and nuts that are specially prepared for this occasion. Some of the common dried fruits often include coconut, pineapple, star fruit and watermelon seeds and candied ginger.

    Sticky rice cakes on the 2nd

    Banh Chung, Sticky rice cake, is a traditional Vietnamese rice cake. It is originated from the story of Lang Lieu, who was a prince of 16th Hung Dynasty. According to the stories, Lang Lieu created Banh Chung in a competition with his siblings for the successor of the king. Banh Chung comes in 2 forms: square form (Banh Chung) which symbolizes the Earth and round form (Banh Giay) which symbolizes the Sun.

    Braised pork with eggs on the 3rd

    Vietnamese typically don't cook during the first three days of the new year. Food for the entire holiday season has been prepared upfront. One of the common kinds is braised pork with eggs (or a big pot of braised pork with eggs) that feeds the Vietnamese family in the rest of the holiday season, from the 3rd to the 6th of January.
