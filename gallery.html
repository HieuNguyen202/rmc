When to eat?

Breakfast is a must!

Vietnamese always make breaskfast a big deal. It is an important meal that provides us energy for a productive morning. In big cities such as Sai Gon, breakfast is commonly served in food stands on the street. There are a variety of delicious food you can choose from: Banh Mi, Bun Bo Hue, Com Som, Xoi.

Dusty Lunch

Eating lunch is sometimes called Com Bui (disty rice). As its name suggests, it is very convinient to grab a quick meal from a nearby food place on the street. After lunch, there is tpyically an hour break, when people take a naps to re-charge for the rest of the day.

King's dinner

Dinner is a big meal. It is usually prepared by the women of the family. A typical Vietnamese dinner consists a couple of dishses that are shared by everyone while each person has their own bowl of rice. The dishes might vary but usualy fall into one of these categories: soup, braised, and stir. It is completely acceptable to raise your bowl up to your mouth and scoop your the food into your mouth using chopsticks.

Fish Sauce

Fish Sauce is a must at every Vietnamese table, no matter what is served. You can use this condiment for dipping meat, seafood and vegetables, and for drizzling on rice. When serving it with steamed meats (such as steamed chicken), I often reduce the water by half so the sauce is more concentrated.
